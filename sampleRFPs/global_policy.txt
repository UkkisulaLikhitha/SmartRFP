RFP Generation Policy Document
1. Purpose

This policy establishes the governance, compliance, and operational framework for generating Request for Proposal (RFP) documents within the organization. It ensures consistency, regulatory adherence, and efficient resource allocation.
2. Governance Framework

    Ownership: The Procurement Office is the custodian of all RFP templates and processes.

    Approval: All RFPs must be reviewed by the Legal & Compliance team before release.

    Version Control: Each RFP must carry a unique reference number and version history.

    Transparency: Vendors must receive identical information; no preferential disclosures.

    Audit Trail: All RFP drafts, approvals, and communications must be logged for 7 years.

3. Compliance Requirements

    Regulatory Adherence: RFPs must comply with local procurement laws and industry regulations.

    Security Standards: All IT-related RFPs must reference ISO 27001, SOC 2, or equivalent.

    Data Residency: Requirements must specify regional data storage obligations.

    Accessibility: RFPs must be published in formats accessible to all vendors (PDF/DOCX).

4. GDPR & Data Protection

    Personal Data Minimization: RFPs must avoid unnecessary collection of personal data.

    Vendor Data Handling: Any vendor information collected must be encrypted (AES‑256).

    Retention Policy: Vendor submissions retained for 5 years, then securely destroyed.

    Consent: Vendors must acknowledge GDPR compliance in their responses.

    Cross‑Border Transfers: Explicitly prohibited unless covered by Standard Contractual Clauses.

5. Operational Capacity & Work Hours

    Maximum Work Hours: Staff involved in RFP generation may not exceed 45 hours per week.

    Minimum Staffing: At least 2 reviewers must validate each RFP before release.

    Maximum Capacity: No more than 10 concurrent RFPs may be in drafting stage.

    Minimum Capacity: At least 1 RFP cycle must be completed per quarter to maintain readiness.

    Overtime Controls: Overtime requires written approval from the Procurement Director.

6. Quality Assurance

    Checklist Validation: Each RFP must pass a standardized checklist (scope, compliance, pricing, evaluation criteria).

    Peer Review: Drafts must be reviewed by a second author to ensure accuracy.

    Vendor Feedback Loop: Post‑RFP surveys must be conducted to improve future processes.

7. Enforcement & Exceptions

    Non‑Compliance: Any deviation from this policy must be reported to the Compliance Office.

    Exceptions: May be granted only by the Chief Procurement Officer with documented justification.

    Disciplinary Action: Repeated violations may result in suspension of RFP drafting privileges.

8. Review Cycle

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Governance Committee to ensure alignment with evolving regulations and organizational needs.
